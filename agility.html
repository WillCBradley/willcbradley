Agility

Getting into medical school is hard.

It requires scoring well in predefined tests, in predictable environments, regardless of the relevance of those tests to the real world.

The result?

The typical med student is extremely good at making themselves more risk-averse, focused, and obedient.

This has obvious advantages in a world where memorisation is crucial…

But what happens when computers memorise everything for us?

What happens when technology uproots our assumptions regarding the nature of a good doctor?

And what happens when these changes occur so quickly that it becomes unethical not to implement the newest tools into medical practice?

After all, regulations can only slow things down so much when lives are on the line.

The answer, I believe, is to relearn agility.

Starting from first principles, we can rediscover our ability to think in a fluid, high-agency, multidisciplinary way.

Because if we don’t, something else will.
